Salar Jung Museum Information

History and Background

The Salar Jung Museum was established by Mir Yousuf Ali Khan, also known as Salar Jung III, who was the Prime Minister of the seventh Nizam of Hyderabad. The museum was officially inaugurated on 16th December 1951 by the then Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru. The museum's collection comprises art and artifacts amassed by Salar Jung III, his father, and his grandfather over a period of 35 years.

Collections and Highlights

The museum boasts an extensive collection of sculptures, paintings, carvings, textiles, manuscripts, ceramics, metallic artifacts, carpets, clocks, and furniture from various parts of the world. One of the most notable pieces is the marble statue of "Veiled Rebecca" by Italian sculptor Giovanni Maria Benzoni. The jade collection includes remarkable artifacts such as daggers belonging to Emperor Jehangir and Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. The museum also features European paintings by renowned artists and Indian art from various states and periods. The Clocks Gallery is particularly popular, showcasing an array of timepieces from around the globe, including a captivating musical clock.

Visitor Information

Located in Darulshifa, Hyderabad, Telangana, the museum welcomes visitors every day except Fridays and public holidays, from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM. There is an entry fee, with concessions for children, students, and senior citizens. Extra charges apply for photography and videography.

Importance

The Salar Jung Museum holds immense cultural significance, serving as a treasure trove of art and history that represents a wide array of cultures and periods. It is an invaluable educational resource, providing insights into the artistic and cultural heritage of India and other parts of the world. As one of the largest museums globally, it attracts visitors from all over, drawn by its vast and diverse collection.
